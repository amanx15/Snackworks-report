Part one

Universal rules

These apply to every Snackworks video, regardless of which product you're creating for. Tap any rule to expand.

01
Must do
#ad overlay — full duration, prominent
+

The single most common reason content gets flagged. #ad must appear on screen in a prominent location for the entire duration — not just at the start.

"#ad" embedded as a text overlay, full duration, easy to spot. Bottom corner is fine.
"#add" (typo), "Ad", or hidden under your watermark. Don't only flash it briefly.
02
Must do
Verbal partnership disclosure in the first 10 seconds
+

You must say out loud that you've partnered with the brand within the first 10 seconds. Captions alone don't count.

"I partnered with OREO…" / "SOUR PATCH KIDS sent me…" / "In collaboration with CHIPS AHOY!"
Skip the verbal callout. Note: brand is OREO singular — never "OREOs".
For ASMR or no-voiceover videos, the disclosure must appear in the caption AND a clear text overlay.
03
Must do
Required hashtags and handles at the start of caption
+

Specific hashtags and handles must appear at the beginning of your caption — and they're different per product. @snackworksofficial and #ad are required for all four. See product cards below for exact tags.

04
Must do
Commercial Music Library only
+

Sound MUST be sourced from TikTok's Commercial Music Library (Pre-cleared Music for Organic Content). Trending songs, music lyrics, third-party audio, photos, artwork, slogans, fonts, or any copyrighted work without proper license = video rejected.

05
Must do
Never consume product directly from the packaging
+

You can show reaching into the bag, you can show eating — but not in the same continuous shot. Always cut between them.

Reach into bag → CUT → eating shot. The edit shows portion control.
Pull product straight from the bag to your mouth on camera. Don't drink/pour from a bowl either.
06
Must do
One serving max — check the nutrition label
+

Showing or consuming more than one serving = automatic flag. Big piles imply overconsumption — even if you don't eat them all.

Quick reference: SPK Chews bundle = 7–9 pieces per serving. CHIPS AHOY! = 2 cookies. When in doubt, look at the label.
07
Must do
No overindulgence or "craving" / "addicting" language
+

Anything promoting overindulgence, mukbang content, or addictive framing is a hard no.

"So good" / "Really enjoyed these" / "A great snack"
"I could eat the whole pack" / "Craving" / "Addicting" / "Get your fix" / Mukbang
08
Must do
No superlatives — "perfect", "ideal", "ultimate"
+

The brand prohibits absolute or superlative adjectives describing the product itself. Using them for an occasion is fine.

"Perfect for movie night" / "Perfect for a watch party" (occasion-based)
"Perfect amount of creme" / "Perfect amount of sour" / "Ideal flavor" / "Ultimate cookie"
09
Must do
No unapproved health claims
+

The brand does not position products as healthy, vegan, clean, low-calorie, or as alternatives that help with health conditions.

Talk about taste, fun, occasions, sharing, flavor.
"Healthy" / "Vegan" / "Clean" / "Guilt-free" / Reference any health condition
10
Must do
No false pricing or urgency claims
+
Mention current discount only if confirmed active. Use neutral language.
"Last day of the deal" / "Won't find a cheaper price in store" / "Only until midnight"
11
Heads up
No third-party logos, products, or copyrights
+
Plain backgrounds. Generic clothing. Cover tattoos if no IP rights.
Competitor mentions, branded apparel (Nike, Gymshark, sports teams), branded electronics, alcohol, drugs, violence.
12
Heads up
No art in the background
+

Paintings, prints, posters, magnets, murals — all copyright concerns unless you own the rights. Plain walls or blurred backgrounds only.

13
Heads up
No identifiable people without a written release
+

Anyone identifiable on camera — friends, family, partners, kids, bystanders — needs a written release. Children under 13 cannot consume or interact with the product.

14
Heads up
No movies, events, celebrities, or cultural moments
+

Don't reference movies, event titles, celebrity names, taglines, or social/cultural moments. This includes the World Cup — explicit rule for the Chips Ahoy! bundle.

15
Heads up
No unsafe handling or unhygienic food practices
+
Open with hands or rounded scissors. Use food-safe surfaces.
Open fire (incl. candles). Microwave packages. Bench scrapers. Catch-in-mouth tricks.
16
Heads up
No pets, kids<13, or animals near product
+

Don't show pets or animals eating, or in a position to eat, Mondelēz products. Don't show children under 13 consuming or interacting with product. Off-camera reactions count.

17
Heads up
No religion, politics, or sensitive topics
+

No references to religion, politics, racism, gender, or sexual inequality. No negative or disparaging statements about any company or product. No foul language, no overt sexual content.

18
Good to know
No third-party recipes or ingredient amounts
+

Don't use someone else's recipe or imagery as your own. Don't include ingredient amounts or serving suggestions in recipe content.

Content ideas to play with
Macro shot opener
Open with a close-up of the candy. Capture the colors and unique shapes — visual variety alone earns a rewatch.
"Crowning my favorite flavor"
Show candies lined up, emphasize differences, crown a winner. Bring in a guest (with consent) — works especially well with parents (generational contrast) or first-timers.
"Make a candy salad with me"
Pour the contents into a container and mix. Emphasize variety and the sweet-sour balance. Suggest sharing occasions. Avoid eating candy on camera.
A few brand things to remember
  • The chews have SOUR on the inside, NOT "sugar". Saying "sugar in the middle" is wrong.
  • The red flavor is Redberry, NOT "strawberry".
